PART C
Functional Specification
Backend systems, user roles, and technical requirements for the developer
DEVELOPER / DESIGNER NOTE — NOT FOR PUBLICATION
This entire section is system specification. None of this content appears on the live site. It is addressed to the developer and should be removed from any client-facing version of this document.

C.1  Platform Recommendation
Recommended stack
WordPress with a custom theme. Plugins: WP Job Manager or Directories Pro (directory and listing backend), WooCommerce (marketplace transactions), MemberPress or ProfilePress (vendor accounts and dashboards), Advanced Custom Fields (custom listing fields), Gravity Forms or WS Form (verification and enquiry forms).
Alternative stack
Next.js with a headless CMS (Sanity or Contentful) and Supabase backend. Higher build cost; better long-term scalability. Recommended if the developer has strong React experience.
Do not use
Webflow — cannot handle vendor dashboards natively. Wix or Squarespace — insufficient for multi-role user management and custom listing workflows.
Payment integration
Paystack (primary): GHS, card, and mobile money. Stripe (secondary): international card payments and USD transactions for Snug Haven. Paystack has a native WooCommerce plugin. Commission tracking is manual in Phase 1.
Hosting
VPS or managed WordPress host: Kinsta, WP Engine, or a DigitalOcean droplet. Not shared hosting.
Search
Algolia or Elasticsearch for directory, property, marketplace, and guide search at scale.
Email
Transactional email via SendGrid or Postmark. Branded templates for all system emails.

C.3  Vendor Onboarding & Approval Flow
This flow applies identically across all listing sections. The verification form fields differ by section.

Step 1 — Registration
Vendor completes the registration form: business name, contact details, section (Directory / Real Estate / Marketplace / Snug Haven partner), category, website or social proof, brief description. KYC verification submitted. Account created with Pending status. No listing access at this stage.
Step 2 — Verification form
Vendor is prompted immediately after registration to complete the section-specific verification form and upload supporting KYC documents.
Step 3 — Admin review
Admin receives a notification. Reviews registration and verification. Three options: Approve (vendor receives confirmation email and dashboard access), Reject (vendor receives explanation email with reapplication guidance), or Request more information (application held, query sent by email).
Step 4 — Dashboard access
Approved vendor logs in to the dashboard. Can create, edit, pause, and renew listings within their approved category. Cannot publish without completing all required fields.
Step 5 — Listing publication
New listings from approved vendors are published immediately on field completion. Admin receives a notification and can flag or remove at any time. Flagged listings are paused automatically pending admin decision.
Step 6 — Renewals
Housing listings: 30-day active period. Directory subscriptions: monthly. Vendor receives automated renewal reminders at 7 days and 3 days before expiry. Renewal is self-serve via dashboard.

C.4  Admin Dashboard — Required Views
	•	Pending vendor applications, sorted by date submitted
	•	All active listings, filterable by section, category, tier, vendor, and date
	•	Flagged listings awaiting admin decision
	•	Rejected applications with reason logged
	•	Vendor communications inbox (direct messaging)
	•	Commission tracking: Communly-referred enquiries, confirmed lets or sales, invoices raised
	•	Revenue summary: Verified Badge subscriptions, featured placements, listing fees, commission earned

DEVELOPER / DESIGNER NOTE — NOT FOR PUBLICATION
Admin dashboard must not be accessible via any public-facing URL. Use /admin with IP restriction or a separate subdomain: admin.ghanaexpats.com. All admin actions are logged with timestamp and admin user ID for audit purposes.

C.5  Verification Forms by Section
Common base fields — all sections
	•	Legal business or individual name
	•	Trading name if different
	•	Primary contact: name, phone, WhatsApp, email
	•	Business address or operating area
	•	Years in operation
	•	Brief business description (200 words max)

	•	Declaration: I confirm the above is accurate and I am authorised to list this business (checkbox)

Real Estate — additional fields
	•	Agent licence number or registration body (optional for individual landlords)
	•	Company registration number (agencies)
	•	Property types: residential rental, commercial rental, short-let, sale, or combination
	•	Average number of active listings
	•	Service area: neighbourhoods or regions
	•	Proof of ownership or agency mandate (document upload: ID plus at minimum one property document)
	•	Agreement to commission terms: 10–15% on Communly-referred completed lets or sales (tick-box with link to commission agreement)

Marketplace — additional fields
	•	Product or service category
	•	Description of what will be listed
	•	For physical goods: confirmation of ability to ship or arrange local collection
	•	For food vendors: valid food handling certificate (document upload)
	•	Payment methods accepted
	•	Returns and refund policy (150 words max)

Verified Directory — additional fields
	•	Professional body registration, if applicable
	•	Insurance or bonding status (required for healthcare, legal, security categories)
	•	Agreement to a Communly vetting call (required for Verified tier)
	•	Preferred tier: Free or Communly Verified (GH₵500 setup + GH₵250/month)

Snug Haven partner property — additional fields
	•	Property type: private room, ensuite room, serviced apartment, full house, guesthouse
	•	Number of rooms or units
	•	Location and neighbourhood
	•	Amenities checklist: A/C, internet speed, generator backup, security, parking, workspace, kitchen access
	•	Minimum 5 honest photographs: exterior, room, bathroom, kitchen or common area, neighbourhood
	•	Nightly and monthly rates in USD
	•	Minimum stay requirement
	•	Availability calendar or confirmation that real-time availability will be maintained
	•	Agreement to Snug Haven Collection standards and vetting visit (Communly team conducts a physical or video inspection before approval)


C.6  Technical Requirements
Mobile-first
All pages, forms, and dashboards must function flawlessly at 375px minimum viewport width. The majority of community members access on mobile.
Page speed
Core Web Vitals pass required on all public-facing pages. Compress all images at upload. Lazy-load non-critical assets.
Analytics
Google Analytics 4 on all pages. Event tracking on: directory searches, form submissions, guide PDF downloads, CTA clicks, WhatsApp button clicks, listing views, vendor registrations, Facebook Group join clicks.
SEO
Clean URL structure as per sitemap (Part A). Schema markup: LocalBusiness for directory listings, Product for marketplace, RealEstateListing for property listings. Breadcrumb navigation. Meta title and description editable per page.
Security
SSL throughout. CAPTCHA on all public-facing forms. Rate limiting on registration and login endpoints. Admin panel IP-restricted or on a separate subdomain.
Accessibility
WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Sufficient colour contrast on all text. All forms keyboard-navigable. Alt text on all images.
Emergency UI
Phone numbers and dispatch buttons on /emergency-response must be oversized and tap-friendly on mobile (minimum 56px touch target). No modals or multi-step flows at the point of action — one tap, one outcome.
Subscription billing
Verified Badge monthly subscriptions handled via Paystack recurring billing. Vendor dashboard shows next billing date and payment history.

[ SECTION OVERVIEW ]
Doing business in Ghana involves navigating a specific regulatory landscape. This section covers what you need to know and connects you to verified professionals who can execute it.

Business Registration
The Ghana Investment Promotion Centre (GIPC) governs foreign business registration. Minimum capital requirements and sector restrictions apply. A verified corporate attorney or GIPC-registered agent can manage the process from document preparation to certificate issuance.
Statutory Compliance
All operating businesses in Ghana are subject to: the Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) for tax registration and filing, the Social Security and National Insurance Trust (SSNIT) for employer contributions, and sector-specific licensing from bodies such as the Ghana Tourism Authority (GTA) and the Ghana Immigration Service (GIS) where applicable.

Due Diligence
Before entering any joint venture, acquiring property, or signing a commercial lease, independent due diligence is essential. Our directory lists verified corporate advisory and legal professionals who specialise in Ghana-market transactions.
